|History=
The KV-220 externally differed from ordinary KVs by the greater length of the hull, an increase of one support roller in the track, and a new turret with the 85 mm F-30 Main Gun. R38 KV-220 beta  The weapon was specially designed for arming this tank in the design bureau of Plant No. R38 KV-220 beta  92, under the supervision of Grabin. R38 KV-220 beta  It was tested successfully in the T-28 Medium Tank in the fall of 1940. R38 KV-220 beta  Because of the steep growth in the weight of the tank, which now reached 62 tons, the V-5 engine with 700 h. R38 KV-220 beta p. R38 KV-220 beta  was mounted. R38 KV-220 beta  Testing of the KV-220 commenced on 30 January 1941 and was halted the very next day because of engine failure. R38 KV-220 beta 
In contrast to artillerymen, tankers were falling far behind in the development of a new heavy tank. R38 KV-220 beta  Therefore they decided to employ the KV-220 to accelerate the work of testing the new assemblies and aggregates of the KV-3. R38 KV-220 beta  On 20 April 1941 a new engine was finally mounted in the KV-220 and the tank, loaded up to 70 tons, the loaded weight of a KV-3, was submitted for testing. R38 KV-220 beta  By the end of May 1941 it had been driven 1330 kilometres. R38 KV-220 beta  In the report of the plant test personnel it was noted that the tank had "a poor-shifting transmission, the support roller spindles and suspension arms had bent, the torsion bars had twisted, and the engine lacked sufficient power for a 70-ton tank. R38 KV-220 beta "

On 20 May the KV-220 was brought in for repair, in the course of which the vehicle received a V-2SN Diesel Engine with supercharger producing 850 h. R38 KV-220 beta p. R38 KV-220 beta  The tank was subjected to testing again beginning on 30 May and by 22 June 1941 had traveled a total distance of 1,985 km. R38 KV-220 beta  But because of problems that arose during the preparation of the forged turret, the work period was extended. R38 KV-220 beta 

In total, only one prototype was constructed in 1941. R38 KV-220 beta
