|History=

The Jagdpanzer E-100 as it currently is in the game is a vehicle based on proposals to fit a 17 cm gun on the chassis of the E-100 to act as a tank destroyer. G72 JagdPz E100 


==Development of Superheavy Guns==

Two guns were developed by the Krupp design bureau in late March of 1944, the 17 cm StuK L/53 and the 15 cm StuK L/63. G72 JagdPz E100  According to calculations, both could penetrate 200 mm of armour at 4 kilometers. G72 JagdPz E100  It is not known who the intended target for these monsters was, but both guns appear in Guderian's report detailing potential developments on April 7th, 1944. G72 JagdPz E100 

The designation of both guns dictated that they were meant for SPGs. G72 JagdPz E100  These guns could potentially be installed on relatively light Panther or Tiger based vehicles, but in reality only one carrier was built, the 17 cm Kanone 72 (Sfl) Geschuetzwagen Tiger otherwise known as Grille 17. G72 JagdPz E100  The choice of chassis for gigantic anti-tank guns remained open. G72 JagdPz E100 

==Sturmgeschutz Maus==

Around the same time that Krupp proposed projects for superheavy guns, activity around the superheavy Maus tank resumed, simultaneously with its rival, the E-100, the 140 ton superheavy tank from Adlerwerke. G72 JagdPz E100  In practice, Adler proposed a redesign of the 1942 Tiger-Maus project, developed by Krupp and cancelled in favour of the Maus. G72 JagdPz E100  Even the turret used in the blueprint is the Maus turret from January 1st, 1943, also proposed for the Tiger-Maus. G72 JagdPz E100  This blueprint has notes on two guns, 15 cm and 17 cm. G72 JagdPz E100  In order to not breed any illusions, let me state that this does not mean anyone had plans to install these guns in this tank. G72 JagdPz E100 

An American CIOS report sheds light on this note. G72 JagdPz E100  It states that German engineers did consider the E-100 as a potential platform for 149 mm and 173 mm guns. G72 JagdPz E100  It also states that the results of their calculations were disappointing: these guns could be squeezed in, but the turret would have to become immobile. G72 JagdPz E100  The aforementioned turret design was not even considered. G72 JagdPz E100 

In April of 1944, Krupp started work on a new simplified turret, known as the Maus II turret. G72 JagdPz E100  The E-100 version had its mass reduced to 35 tons by reducing the thickness of side armour to 80 mm. G72 JagdPz E100  The 128 mm KwK L/55 would be the main gun on both turrets, with a secondary 75 mm KwK L/24. G72 JagdPz E100  A rangefinder was also added to both turrets, with the axis of rotation of this pairing directly next to it.
