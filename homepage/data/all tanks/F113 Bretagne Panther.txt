F113 Bretagne Panther  Brawling should be avoided when possible due to fairly low alpha damage and low armor. F113 Bretagne Panther  Hulldown is effective. F113 Bretagne Panther  The turret while not being the smallest target has a very high tendency to bounce shots at long ranges, and the L/70 will make short work of most tanks. F113 Bretagne Panther  Angling is not very effective due to the paper thin 40mm side armor, but can be used against small caliber guns to increase the chances of a bounce. F113 Bretagne Panther  Also due to the fairly large profile, one has to take extra time to make sure the bush they are sitting in (if abusing camo skill and nets) is big enough to hide the entire tank. F113 Bretagne Panther  It is also very hard to avoid enemy fire, since the tank is somewhat large for a medium (comparable to a small heavy), meaning that the only ways to take fire is in a hull down (moderately bouncy turret), or when angled properly with the lower plate preferably hidden (assuming the gun is a low end tier V or most tier IV guns, as anything with more then 130mm of penetration will go through the tank regardless of angle unless it gets a lucky bounce)

|History= 

Panther "Bretagne" was an abandoned German Panther tank revived by the French freedom fighter division Besnier Autonomous Tank Squadron, whose task was to repair deserted German vehicles in the Allied French zone. F113 Bretagne Panther  Two Panthers were recovered by the division, one being named "Bretagne" and the other either "Auvergne" or "Dauphiné", depending on which source one believes. F113 Bretagne Panther  The tanks were most likely used for training purposes by French troops in 1945. F113 Bretagne Panther  One of the Panthers (unknown which) was restored to running condition in 2017 and appeared at TankFest 2019. F113 Bretagne Panther
